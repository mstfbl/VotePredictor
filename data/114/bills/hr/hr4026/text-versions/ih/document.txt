                                 A BILL


 
   To provide that a concealed handgun license shall be treated as a 
verifying identity document for purposes of aircraft passenger security 
 screening, and to prohibit the Federal Government from collecting or 
storing information about an individual relating to a concealed handgun 
                                license.

    Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the 
United States of America in Congress assembled,

SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

    This Act may be cited as the ``Nondiscriminatory Transportation 
Screening Act'' or the ``TSA Act''.

SEC. 2. TREATMENT OF CONCEALED HANDGUN LICENSE AS VERIFYING IDENTITY 
              DOCUMENT FOR PURPOSES OF AIRCRAFT PASSENGER SECURITY 
              SCREENING.

    For purposes of part 1560 of title 49, Code of Federal Regulations, 
a concealed handgun license shall be treated as a verifying identity 
document.

SEC. 3. PROHIBITION ON FEDERAL RECORDKEEPING RELATING TO CONCEALED 
              HANDGUN LICENSES.

    The Federal Government may not collect or store information about 
an individual relating to a concealed handgun license.

SEC. 4. CONCEALED HANDGUN LICENSE DEFINED.

    In this Act the term ``concealed handgun license'' means a license, 
permit, or other document issued by a State or a political subdivision 
of a State that authorizes an individual to carry a concealed handgun.
