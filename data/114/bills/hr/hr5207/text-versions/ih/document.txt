                                 A BILL


 
 To amend the Immigration and Nationality Act to provide that an alien 
may not be denied admission to the United States because of the alien's 
                   religion, and for other purposes.

    Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the 
United States of America in Congress assembled,

SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

    This Act may be cited as the ``Freedom of Religion Act of 2016''.

SEC. 2. PROHIBITION ON DENYING ADMISSION BECAUSE OF RELIGION.

    Chapter 2 of title I of the Immigration and Nationality Act (8 
U.S.C. 1181 et seq.) is amended by inserting after section 219 the 
following:

``SEC. 220. PROHIBITION ON DENYING ADMISSION BECAUSE OF RELIGION.

    ``Notwithstanding any other provision of the immigration laws, an 
alien may not be denied admission to the United States because of the 
alien's religion or lack of religious beliefs.''.
